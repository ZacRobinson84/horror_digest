Michael Sarnoski's [*The Death of Robin Hood*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3955863/the-death-of-robin-hood-review/) strips the legend down to its final, violent days—a gritty inversion of the outlaw myth from the director of *A Quiet Place: Day One*.

Ben Young (*Hounds of Love*) is directing Stephen King's [*Mister Yummy*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3955816/stephen-kings-mister-yummy-adaptation-on-the-way-from-hounds-of-love-director/), adapting the short story from *The Bazaar of Bad Dreams*. King fans know Young's sensibilities—bleak and uncompromising.

A Lovecraftian angle worth tracking: [*Lenskeeper*](https://rue-morgue.com/keep-your-eye-on-the-lovecraftian-fright-feature-lenskeeper-trailer-poster-release-info/) takes inspiration from Fulci and Barker alongside the cosmic horror playbook. Details sparse, but the trailer's worth a look.

Kevin Lewis (*Willy's Wonderland*) is back with [*Mercy Good*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3955782/mercy-good-stephen-lang-starring-in-salem-horror-movie-from-willys-wonderland-director/), a Salem-set supernatural feature starring Stephen Lang. A family relocates to a historic home tied to something centuries-old waiting underneath.

Indonesia's taking a crack at remaking [*Gonjiam: Haunted Asylum*](https://ihorror.com/gonjiam-haunted-asylum-getting-remake/), the found-footage film widely considered one of the best in the subgenre. Could go either way, but Indonesia's horror output has earned the benefit of the doubt.

Josh Heaps's debut [*City Wide Fever*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3955812/lo-fi-giallo-city-wide-fever-heads-to-midnight-pulp-next-month/) traps a film student in a giallo nightmare via USB drive—meta, lo-fi, hitting Midnight Pulp July 7. Exactly the kind of niche thing worth catching early.

An 1982 Euro-schlock gem: [*Der Fan*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3955824/der-fan-4k-restoration-heads-to-theaters/) (*Trance*) gets its first U.S. theatrical run with a new 4K restoration, premiering at Metrograph NYC on August 28. The pop-star obsession nightmare has been circulating in bootlegs for decades.
