André Øvredal's [*Passenger*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950358/passenger-bloody-disgusting-presents-free-sneak-screenings-of-andre-ovredals-scariest-movie-yet/) lands a road-trip couple with something unwanted in tow. Øvredal calls it his scariest film yet, which carries weight given *The Autopsy of Jane Doe* and *The Last Voyage of the Demeter*. Paramount's handling the release.

Avalon Fast's [*Camp*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950453/camp-trailer-gen-zs-answer-to-the-craft-is-a-dreamy-horror-hangout-movie/) drops June 26 via Dark Sky Films. A sapphic coming-of-age horror about witchcraft and grief that wears its influences visibly but sounds like it earns them.

[*Never After Dark*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950460/never-after-dark-sets-september-theatrical-release/), a Japanese ghost story from Dave Boyle, hits theaters September 25. Magnolia Pictures gets the wide release.

Javier Bardem as [Max Cady](https://rue-morgue.com/official-trailer-for-the-new-cape-fear-arrives-with-a-vengeance/) in Apple TV's *Cape Fear* remake. Nick Antosca directing. The trailer looks solid—Bardem's gone full menace.

A taxidermist wears his boss's skin in [*Killing Kelly*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950386/dark-comedy-thriller-killing-kelly-casting/), an Ireland-set dark comedy from *Hackers* screenwriter Evan Rachel Wood. Thomasin McKenzie and Jason Isaacs star. Shooting starts this fall in Dublin.

Freida McFadden's [*The Divorce*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950378/the-housemaid-authors-new-novel-the-divorce-optioned-for-film-adaptation/)—her follow-up to *The Housemaid*'s $400M worldwide haul—is optioned by Studiocanal and Working Title. Competitive auction, obvious reasons.

Puppet Combo's [*The Backrooms*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/video-games/3950402/puppet-combo-releasing-their-own-the-backrooms-video-game-trailer/) liminal horror arrives on consoles May 14. Retro aesthetic, which fits the lore.
