André Øvredal's [Passenger](https://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3952877/passenger-review-a-gorgeous-yet-bland-haunted-house-road-trip/) hits theaters tonight with a haunted-road-trip premise, but reviewers say the execution doesn't match the concept—gorgeous cinematography masking narrative drift.

Radio Silence (Ready or Not, Scream) is directing a [Choose Your Own Adventure](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952839/choose-your-own-adventure-movie-in-the-works-from-scream-directors-radio-silence/) adaptation at 20th Century Studios. Details sparse on how they'll translate branching narratives to film.

Kyle Gallner joins Brie Larson and J.J. Abrams in [Skeletons](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952838/kyle-gallner-joins-brie-larson-in-jt-mollners-creature-feature-skeletons/), JT Mollner's creature feature. Gallner reunites with Mollner after Strange Darling; the film's told from a young boy's perspective discovering something about his family.

Dev Patel wrapped [The Peasant](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952748/a24-wraps-dev-patel-revenge-thriller-the-peasant-cast-revealed/), an A24 period revenge thriller he directed and stars in. Cast includes Christian Friedel and Sebastian Bull; details on the plot remain thin.

Natalie Erika James on [Saccharine](https://bloody-disgusting.com/interviews/3952591/natalie-erika-james-talks-body-horror-diet-culture-and-saccharine/): the filmmaker discusses body horror, fatphobia, and diet culture in an [interview](https://bloody-disgusting.com/interviews/3952591/natalie-erika-james-talks-body-horror-diet-culture-and-saccharine/) that digs deeper than usual genre coverage.

Léa Mysius brought home-invasion thriller [The Birthday Party](https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/573629/home-invasion-thriller-the-birthday-party-invades-cannes/) to Cannes. Centered on a celebration gone catastrophically wrong.
