Yeon Sang-ho's new [infection horror](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951147/dozens-of-zombies-infect-official-poster-for-train-to-busan-directors-colony/) *Colony* hits theaters in August alongside a *Train to Busan* 10th anniversary re-release. After a decade of zombie films that barely broke a sweat, a return to the director who actually understood the form.

Michelle Garza Cervera (*Huesera*) and Isa Mazzei (*Cam*) are [adapting Laura van den Berg's](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951161/huesera-director-faces-of-death-writer-to-adapt-horror-novel-the-third-hotel/) psychological horror novel *The Third Hotel*—a pairing that suggests actual ambition for the source material.

Panos Cosmatos' [*Flesh of the Gods*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950958/panos-cosmatos-flesh-of-the-gods-enters-production-esme-creed-miles-roland-moller-alba-baptista-join-cast/) enters production with Kristen Stewart and Wagner Moura in an '80s vampire thriller written by Andrew Kevin Walker. *Mandy* follow-up from the guy who understands color and excess better than anyone working.

[Jack Taylor](https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/572306/actor-jack-taylor-of-count-dracula-pieces-and-many-others-has-passed-away/), the Spanish character actor who haunted European horror from the '70s onward—*Pieces*, *Count Dracula*, Jess Franco's entire filmography—has died at 99. A genre fixture for half a century.

Gary Dauberman's production company acquired rights to Nat Cassidy's [*Rest Stop*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951082/gary-dauberman-to-produce-nat-cassidys-rest-stop-adaptation-green-room-meets-geralds-game/) novella for adaptation. One week after publication. The description—*Green Room* meets *Gerald's Game*—feels like marketing speak, but Cassidy's work doesn't usually disappoint.

Roar Uthaug (*Troll*) is developing [*Amara*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951090/folkloric-curse-horror-movie-amara-in-development-from-troll-director/), a folkloric curse film set in Thailand about backpackers tangling with something older. Reuniting him with *Troll* screenwriter Espen Aukan. Details sparse but the premise pulls harder than most Netflix horror.

Alexandre O. Philippe's [*In Search of Nosferatu*](https://rue-morgue.com/patton-oswalt-joins-alexandre-o-philippes-in-search-of-nosferatu-as-executive-producer/) is rolling. The doc/restoration project on Murnau's lost masterpiece kicks off with Patton Oswalt as executive producer, principal photography underway at Cannes.
