Nicolas Winding Refn's long-gestating [Maniac Cop remake](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3953056/maniac-cop-nicolas-winding-refn-announces-fresh-take-on-1980s-slasher-movie-coming-soon/) is finally moving forward as a feature through MUBI and Goodfellas, after years bouncing between formats. No timeline yet.

Jayro Bustamante (La Llorona, Rita) is directing [República Luminosa](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952990/republica-luminosa-jayro-bustamantes-next-horror-movie-unleashes-feral-children/), adapting Andrés Barba's novel about a remote island town terrorized by 32 feral children emerging from the jungle. Details sparse.

Dario Argento announced a [new trilogy](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952919/flesh-of-my-flesh-dario-argento-has-announced-a-brand-new-trilogy-of-upcoming-horror-movies/) at Cannes this week, starting with Flesh of My Flesh—three mothers investigating their missing children. Still in early stages; Argento is 85.

Sam Raimi is [remaking Magic](https://www.dreadcentral.com/editorials/573772/remembering-the-magic-sam-raimi-set-to-remake-1978-cult-classic-video/), William Goldman's 1978 ventriloquist-dummy nightmare. Raimi helming a straight redo of a cult classic is unusual for him; worth watching.

Henry Chaisson (Antlers, "Servant") makes his feature directorial debut on [Recluse](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952911/recluse-first-look-at-nightmare-fuel-horror-movie-from-the-writer-of-antlers/), a gothic thriller with Sasha Frolova. Steven Schneider (Paranormal Activity) producing. Premiered at Tribeca.

Ryan Spindell will write and direct the [feature adaptation of The Upturned Stone](https://dailydead.com/horror-highlights-the-upturned-stone-feature-film-adaptation-wet-paper-bag-trailer-release-info/), Scott Hampton's graphic novel—often called "the scariest comic ever written"—with Cullen Bunn co-writing the screenplay.

Romain Gavras (Athena) is directing [Sacrifice](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952981/sacrifice-first-look-anya-taylor-joy-plays-a-doomsday-cult-leader-in-netflix-movie/) for Netflix, a genre-bender with Anya Taylor-Joy as a doomsday cult leader and Chris Evans. Premiere date TBA.
