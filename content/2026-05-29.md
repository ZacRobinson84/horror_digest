Scott Glosserman's [*Behind the Mask II: The Return of Leslie Vernon*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/editorials/3953738/behind-the-mask-ii-the-return-of-leslie-vernon-wraps-up-a-wildly-successful-fundraising-campaign/) crushed its Kickstarter goal in 10 minutes and wrapped with $400,000. The meta-slasher sequel has been gestating for years; the funding blitz suggests appetite still exists for it.

Kane Parsons' [*Backrooms*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3953720/backrooms-review-a-disturbing-liminal-voyage-through-the-human-mind/) expands his YouTube series into a feature debut. The liminal horror cycle continues—same DNA as *Exit 8* and *The Space Between* (which wrapped production in a Midwestern mall with *Haunt* director Damian Maffei). Verdict: depends on whether you're still interested in infinite beige hallways.

Grindhouse Releasing is [bringing *Cannibal Holocaust*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3953627/cannibal-holocaust-heads-to-theaters-with-version-youve-never-seen-from-grindhouse-releasing/) back to theaters in a restored special edition. Deodato's 1980 notorious is hitting multiplex screens—not a 4K restoration of something obscure, but an actual theatrical run of the thing most people have only encountered in legend.

Sarah Langan, Bram Stoker winner, has a new novel called [*Trad Wife*](https://rue-morgue.com/exclusive-interview-author-sarah-langan-turns-conservative-culture-into-nightmare-fuel-in-trad-wife/) mining contemporary conservative culture for horror material. Her angle: online obsession and curated femininity as nightmare fuel.

Zac Efron is [headlining an *Angel Heart* series](https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/574448/zac-efron-to-star-in-and-produce-angel-heart-series-for-hbo-and-a24/) for A24 and HBO. This adapts William Hjortsberg's 1978 novel—the same source as the 1987 neo-noir *Angel Heart* (De Niro, Corey), now getting the prestige-TV treatment. Details sparse.

Mexican directors Arturo and Roy Ambriz (del Toro protégés) made [*I Am Frankelda*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3953641/i-am-frankelda-trailer-mexicos-first-ever-stop-motion-movie-is-the-handmade-cure-for-ai-slop/), billed as Mexico's first feature-length stop-motion film. Coming to Netflix this June. The trailer emphasizes handmade craft as counterpoint to generative image spam.

NEON's [*Leviticus*](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3953685/leviticus-new-poster-for-neons-haunting-heartbreaking-horror-movie/) (queer supernatural horror, June 19) and *Sunken Meadow* (North Road's pilot spec—family drama meets elevated horror, plot under wraps) both landed without much flesh on the bone yet.
