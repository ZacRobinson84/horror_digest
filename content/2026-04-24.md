Jeff Nichols' [King Snake](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947775/jeff-nichols-gothic-horror-king-snake-with-margaret-qualley-michael-shannon-enters-production/) is in production in Arkansas—Southern gothic horror with Margaret Qualley and Drew Starkey inheriting a farm that comes with both literal and metaphysical demons. Nichols directed *Mud* and *Take Shelter*, so this isn't a casual project.

Casper Kelly's [Buddy](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947762/too-many-cooks-director-s-buddy-set-for-september-theatrical-release/) lands in theaters September 4 via Roadside Attractions. A satirical horror film about a killer unicorn mascot from the director of *Too Many Cooks* and *V/H/S/Halloween*—details are sparse but the premise alone justifies the acquisition.

[The Backrooms](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947852/enter-the-backrooms-with-liminal-horror-image-gallery/) adds Chiwetel Ejiofor and Renate Reinsve to Kane Parsons' feature debut based on the creepypasta-turned-YouTube series. The liminal horror premise has 190 million views worth of built-in audience; Parsons is making a real film out of it.

James Gunn confirms [Clayface](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947839/clayface-isnt-a-superhero-horror-movie-its-a-straight-up-horror-movie/) is a straight horror movie, not a superhero hybrid. Tom Rhys Harries plays the disfigured actor turned killer. October release.

[Behind the Mask II](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947688/behind-the-mask-ii-the-return-of-leslie-vernon-immediately-smashed-through-its-fundraising-goal/) hit its Kickstarter goal in under ten minutes. The 2006 original has aged well; a sequel with actual funding behind it is worth tracking.

Lee Cronin (Evil Dead Rise) discusses his [mummy film](https://dailydead.com/interview-lee-cronin-breaks-down-the-personal-experiences-and-complex-characters-that-drive-his-terrifying-mummy-movie/) in an interview—produced by James Wan, built on the question "what about making a really terrifying mummy movie?" Character-driven rather than spectacle-chasing.

A new indie project [Goat Sword](https://rue-morgue.com/black-metal-vampires-are-coming-in-goat-sword/) shoots later this year in NYC: black metal vampires pitched as *What We Do in the Shadows* meets *Lords of Chaos*.
