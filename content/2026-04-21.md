Jaume Collet-Serra's [Play Dead](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947150/netflix-picks-up-play-dead-ww-ii-survival-thriller-from-jaume-collet-serra-sam-raimi/) — a WWII survival thriller pitched as a mashup of Don't Breathe and 1917 — is heading to Netflix. Sam Raimi producing. Collet-Serra's track record (House of Wax, Orphan, The Shallows) suggests this will lean into visceral tension rather than spectacle.

Lee Cronin's [The Mummy](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947049/lee-cronins-the-mummy-opening-weekend-box-office-hits-13-5-million-domestic/) opened to $13.5 million domestic. The Evil Dead Rise director's Blumhouse riff on mummy mythology landed with a respectable if unremarkable footprint. [Originally had a much darker ending](https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/569139/lee-cronins-the-mummy-originally-had-a-much-darker-ending/), apparently.

Damian McCarthy's [Hokum](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947125/hokum-final-trailer-opens-closed-minds-to-damian-mccarthys-witchy-nightmare-fuel/) hits theaters May 1. Adam Scott plays a reclusive novelist trapped in a remote Irish inn where a witch haunts the honeymoon suite. McCarthy made Caveat; expect austere, creeping dread.

Jane Schoenbrun (I Saw the TV Glow) is [publishing a horror novel](https://bloody-disgusting.com/books/3947089/jane-schoenbrun-to-publish-horror-novel-public-access-afterworld-in-october-synopsis-revealed/), Public Access Afterworld, in October via Hogarth. Described as speculative fiction mixed with horror and conspiracy, centered on a dark realm beyond our screens.

Ben Leonberg (Good Boy) is [adapting Grady Hendrix's Ankle Snatcher](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947132/good-boy-director-ben-leonberg-to-adapt-grady-hendrixs-ankle-snatcher-for-sony/) for Sony. Hendrix co-wrote an earlier draft; Leonberg and his Good Boy partner Alex Cannon are rewriting.

RZA's [One Spoon of Chocolate](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3947145/rza-serves-up-violent-revenge-in-one-spoon-of-chocolate-red-band-trailer/) hits May 1 in 35mm select engagements. The Wu-Tang founder's revenge thriller, presented by Tarantino, has the red band trailer energy of a grindhouse picture. Shameik Moore stars.

Roger L. Jackson (Scream) is [joining season five of the Irish horror podcast Petrified](https://www.fangoria.com/petrified-irish-podcast-exclusive-cast-announcement/), launching May 13. Also featured: Nathan Stewart-Jarrett and comedian Gearóid (names sparse on the third).
