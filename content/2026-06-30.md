Zach Cregger's Resident Evil [trailer](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3957832/resident-evil-image-austin-abrams-infected/) traps Austin Abrams with infected passengers on a nonstop survival run. September 18 via Sony.

Robert Eggers' Werwulf [drops its first trailer](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3957689/aaron-taylor-johnson-undergroes-bestial-transformation-in-first-werwulf-trailer/). Aaron Taylor-Johnson transforms in 13th-century England as local folklore becomes real. December 25 via Focus Features.

David Robert Mitchell's They Follow reunites with [Maika Monroe and Disasterpeace](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3957802/they-follow-disasterpeace-score/) for the sequel score. The original crew is back.

Yeon Sang-ho (Train to Busan) returns with Colony, an outbreak film hitting theaters August 28. Details sparse from the key art reveal.

Farbod Ardebili's feature debut, The American Winner, stars Flula Borg as a tech billionaire with a monstrous secret and Troy Kotsur in what's being pitched as The Thing meets American Psycho. Currently in production.

Kane Parsons' Backrooms [heads back to AMC theaters](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3957773/kane-parsons-backrooms-heads-back-to-theaters-this-week-with-extended-cut/) July 3 with an extended cut—15 minutes of bonus footage in what they're calling the Everything Must Go Edition.

You're Dead to Me, a Gen Z slasher [paying tribute to '90s teen horror](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3957797/youre-dead-to-me-trailer-gen-z-slasher-pays-tribute-to-90s-teen-horror/), hits Digital and On Demand July 7 via Dark Star Pictures.

Paul Tremblay's Dead But Dreaming of Electric Sheep is a [daring novel](https://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3957762/dead-but-dreaming-of-electric-sheep-book-review/) pushing back against the AI hype with his usual narrative experimentation and bleakness.
