Dennis Iliadis's [Buzzheart](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952121/buzzheart-trailer-last-house-on-the-left-remake-director-drops-psychological-thriller-tomorrow/) hits Digital tomorrow. Greek-set 1990s psychological thriller about deadly obsession; the trailer's out now.

Nicolas Winding Refn's [Her Private Hell](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951994/her-private-hell-teaser-trailer-sophie-winding-refns-first-film-in-10-years/) premieres at Cannes today with Sophie Thatcher. First feature in a decade—neon-drenched, entity-driven, hitting theaters July 24 via Neon.

Na Hong-jin's [Hope](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951950/hope-teaser-trailer-the-wailing-director-unleashes-cosmic-creatures/) (fresh from Cannes) is a sci-fi thriller about cosmic creatures. Korean production with Hwang Jung-min, Taylor Russell, and Cameron Britton. Sequel already in development.

Yeon Sang-ho's [Colony](https://rue-morgue.com/colony-from-train-to-busans-yeon-sang-ho-sets-north-american-premiere-at-new-york-asian-film-festival-director-comments/) got a standing ovation at Cannes. Zombie reimagining from the Train to Busan director; North American premiere set for New York Asian Film Festival.

Paul Feig is directing [Persona](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952060/paul-feig-to-direct-fatal-attraction-style-thriller-persona-from-arrow-co-creator/), a Marc Guggenheim novella adaptation for 20th Century. Fatal Attraction-style thriller with a modern angle; Feig riding high off The Housemaid's success.

Patricio Valladares (Invoking Yell) is making an [interactive 16mm slasher game](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952113/invoking-yell-director-making-a-live-action-choose-your-own-adventure-slasher-game/)—live-action choose-your-own-adventure analog filmmaking hybrid. Details sparse.

Indian folk horror [Bokshi](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3952099/a-witch-is-born-twice-in-indian-folk-horror-film-bokshi/) wrapped after five years development. Shot across remote Himalayan locations in Sikkim, West Bengal, and Assam. Features Boksirit, a fully developed constructed language by Dutch linguist Jan van Steenbergen, alongside Hindi, English, and Nepali.

Stephen King published a new 6,000-word short story, [Dinah's Hat](https://bloody-disgusting.com/books/3951970/stephen-king-publishes-new-short-story-dinahs-hat/), in The Atlantic's June issue—coastal Florida setting instead of his usual New England.
