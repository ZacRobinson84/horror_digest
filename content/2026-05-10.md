Koji Suzuki, author of *Ring*, [died at 68](https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/571917/ringu-author-koji-suzuki-dead-at-68-his-work-changed-horror-forever/). His *Ringu* trilogy reshaped supernatural horror—the viral curse premise, the inevitability, Sadako herself. Everything that followed owes him.

Jonathan Tiersten, who played Ricky in *Sleepaway Camp*, [died May 5 at 60](https://ihorror.com/sleepaway-camp-knew-before-i-did-the-legacy-johnathan-tiersten-left-on-queer-media/). The film's final revelation hit different for queer audiences in 1983 and hasn't stopped mattering since.

Osgood Perkins produced the [*Backrooms* feature adaptation](https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/571804/did-you-know-osgood-perkins-produced-backrooms-and-mentored-kane-parsons/) from Kane Parsons' analog horror series, and mentored Parsons through development. Details sparse on what that mentorship looked like or the film's status.

Damian McCarthy's [essay on folklore and familial violence](https://www.fangoria.com/hokum-damian-mccarthy-fascination-with-folklore-familial-murder-mysteries/) examines his approach to blending horror with dark humor through a personal lens of loss. His *HOKUM* work in particular uses gallows comedy as a survival mechanism.
