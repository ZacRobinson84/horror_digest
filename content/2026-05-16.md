Sam Raimi directing a [remake of Magic](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951880/sam-raimi-to-direct-killer-dummy-magic-reboot-for-lionsgate/), the 1978 ventriloquist killer-doll film. He's reuniting with Freddy vs. Jason writers Mark Swift and Damian Shannon for what's being billed as a "newly imagined" take. Details sparse, but the pairing alone justifies attention.

Francis Lawrence adapting Christopher Golden's forthcoming supernatural novel [Carry Me to My Grave](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951795/i-am-legends-francis-lawrence-to-adapt-christopher-golden-horror-novel-carry-me-to-my-grave/) for Lionsgate. Lawrence directing and producing under his first-look deal.

[The Servant](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951813/nicholas-hoult-colman-domingo-to-star-in-transgressive-the-servant-remake/), a 1963 Joseph Losey psychological thriller, getting remade with Nicholas Hoult, Colman Domingo, Noah Jupe, and Emma Corrin. Set in 1950s New York. No director attached yet.

Joe Begos writing and directing [They Call Him Zorro](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951739/they-call-him-zorro-joe-begos-to-helm-violent-action-horror-take-on-legendary-vigilante/), a horror take on Johnston McCulley's original Zorro writings. Drawing from the 1919 novel The Curse of Capistrano through a violent, genre lens.

Malcolm McDowell [attached to a new Herbert West: Reanimator](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951766/malcolm-mcdowell-to-star-in-h-p-lovecrafts-herbert-west-reanimator-from-haunting-in-connecticut-writers/) from Adam Simon and Tim Metcalfe (The Haunting in Connecticut writers). Filming in Alton, Illinois.

[Boorman and the Devil](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951755/exorcist-ii-documentary-boorman-and-the-devil-acquired-by-yellow-veil-pictures/), a documentary on the troubled production of Exorcist II, acquired by Yellow Veil Pictures. David Kittredge directing, featuring John Boorman in his own words.

André Øvredal's [Passenger](https://bloody-disgusting.com/podcasts/3951861/passenger-movie-podcast-bloody-fm/) hits theaters May 22. Øvredal calls it his scariest film yet—strong claim given Autopsy of Jane Doe and Scary Stories to Tell in the Dark.
