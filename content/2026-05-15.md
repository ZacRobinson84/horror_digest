Curry Barker's next feature [Anything But Ghosts has wrapped](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951692/anything-but-ghosts-curry-barker-reveals-obsession-follow-up-plot-details/) with a premise as lean as it gets: two con artist ghost hunters fleecing elderly clients. Barker's moving fast after *Obsession*'s theater run.

Pascal Plante follows *Red Rooms* with [King's Daughters](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951654/first-look-at-historical-folk-horror-kings-daughters-from-red-rooms-director/), a 1663-set survival drama about King Louis XIV's orphan women shipment to the colonies, seasoned with folk-horror elements. WTFilms picked it up.

Jane Schoenbrun's [Teenage Sex and Death at Camp Miasma](https://bloody-disgusting.com/news/3951624/jane-schoenbrun-to-tour-with-teenage-sex-and-death-at-camp-miasma-ahead-of-theatrical-run/) heads out on a screening tour this summer before its August 7 Mubi release, hitting Pride festivals and regional circuits from June onward.

Colin Minihan (*Grave Encounters*) is directing [Stung](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951535/justin-long-iris-apatow-will-get-stung-by-grave-encounters-director/), a survival thriller in negotiations with Justin Long and Iris Apatow about four teens trapped during a sleepover by—details sparse, but the title implies insects.

Jeremiah Kipp (*The Mortuary Assistant*) wrapped the supernatural horror [Faceless](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3951555/the-mortuary-assistant-director-encounters-evil-in-cambodian-horror-faceless/), shot on location in Cambodia with YouTube ghost hunters running into something genuinely hostile. Footage is done; release timeline unclear.

A new adaptation of [Herbert West: Reanimator](https://www.dreadcentral.com/news/572777/h-p-lovecrafts-herbert-west-reanimator-begins-shooting-in-june/) starts shooting this June in Alton, Illinois. No director or cast details yet.

[Key of Bones: Curse of the Ghost Pirate](https://ihorror.com/exclusive-key-of-bones-reveals-new-poster-and-cannes-fantastic-pavilion-gala-screening/) is screening at Cannes this weekend as part of the Fantastic Pavilion, a supernatural horror-comedy that's apparently making the festival circuit.
