Pinetown Production returns with [Stoneborn](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950833/stoneborn-first-look-at-south-korean-occult-movie-from-creators-of-exhuma/), an occult mystery from the team behind Exhuma. Details sparse, but the track record speaks for itself.

Jeremy Slater is making his directorial debut with [Summoner](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950905/jeremy-slater-to-make-directorial-debut-with-summoner/), which he's calling "the scariest thing I've ever written." He's also scripting Mortal Kombat III while the second film is still in theaters.

[Crystal Lake](https://bloody-disgusting.com/tv/3950691/crystal-lake-friday-the-13th-tv-series-from-a24-and-peacock-will-premiere-this-october/), the Friday the 13th prequel series, premieres October 15 on Peacock. Brad Caleb Kane showruns, Callum Vinson plays young Jason. This marks Jason's return to screen 17 years after the 2009 remake.

Sean Byrne ([The Loved Ones](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950727/the-mannequin-melissa-leo-starring-in-violent-serial-killer-thriller-from-dangerous-animals-director/)) is directing The Mannequin, a serial killer procedural starring Melissa Leo. Byrne's work has earned a small but devoted following.

[Maribel Verdú](https://bloody-disgusting.com/movie/3950803/under-your-feet-key-art-and-international-teaser-hides-horror-in-the-walls/) (Pan's Labyrinth) inherits an apartment with secrets in Under Your Feet, pitched as "Rosemary's Baby meets Hansel & Gretel." Playing Cannes next, with a North American premiere at LALIFF.

Ryan Coogler's [X-Files reboot](https://bloody-disgusting.com/tv/3950807/amy-madigan-steve-buscemi-and-more-join-ryan-cooglers-the-x-files-reboot-cast/) for Hulu is adding Amy Madigan, Steve Buscemi, Ben Foster, and others in guest roles. If nothing else, the casting depth suggests ambition.

Curry Barker's [Obsession](https://bloody-disgusting.com/reviews/3898624/obsession-tiff-review-curry-barker-terrifies-with-wish-fulfillment-horror/) wrings real terror from a Monkey's Paw setup. The sketch-comedian-turned-filmmaker turned a familiar concept into something genuinely unsettling.

Marvel's [Midnight](https://bloody-disgusting.com/news/3950845/marvel-comics-details-midnight-publishing-line-x-men-fantastic-4-spider-man-horror-stories/) publishing line runs horror takes on X-Men, Fantastic Four, and Spider-Man. Comics, not films, but worth noting if you follow the medium.
